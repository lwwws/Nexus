Just that when you say 'correctly identify true positive cases'  it sounds like the explanation of precision.  Precision tries to make sure that all cases marked as positive by the model are truly positive  in other words correctly identifying true positives.  But recall on the other hand tries to identify all true positive cases  correctly  yes  but it doesn't really care if they're al correct  as long as all existing positive cases are identified  it's good to go.  And that's why it'll result in a trade off with precision because if 10 out of 100 are truly positive  and the model says 50 (including the 10) are positive  the recall will be 100% because the model was able to identify all cases.
